Hamer Welcome Sign


The Hamer Welcome Sign was introduced in the 1990's. It became a welcome landmark to local families. The saying would change on the season or event. Welcome to Hamer, Idaho!

Camas Wildlife Refuge


Camas National Wildlife Refuge provides vital habitat for a variety of migratory birds. Large and small mammals as well as several non-migratory bird species depend on the refuge for habitat.

Hamer's Iconic Monolithic Potato Cellar Dome


A 105-foot by 35-foot potato cellar in Hamer, Idaho, built in the early days of the industry. This dome lies right off the interstate (I-15) exit and serves as a landmark to the Hamer township.

St. Anthony Sand Dunes


The St. Anthony Sand Dunes comprise more than 10,000 acres of quartz sand in the Snake River Plain in Idaho. The dunes can range from 10 feet tall to up to 400 feet tall. They are a popular destination in the summer for sand dune motorsports enthusiasts on ATVs and side-by-side UTVs. The dunes serve as a popular recreational activity for families in Hamer.

Hamer Farms


The farming community in Hamer contrives many of the jobs for local populace. Many of the largest potato farms in America are found in or around Hamer, Idaho.

Mud Lake


Mud Lake is close to Hamer, Idaho and a popular location for local populace. Mud Lake is a 4,500 acre lake found in Jefferson County. Two deep marsh units and numerous shallow flooded wetlands contain vital stopover habitat and nesting habitat for waterfowl and shorebirds.
